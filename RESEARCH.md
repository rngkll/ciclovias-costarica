# Similar Projects and Resources

Here is a list of projects and resources similar to CyclOSM, NACTO Design Guide, and cycle.travel, categorized and with their open-source status noted.

## 1. OSM-based Bicycle Maps

These projects render bicycle-specific map layers using data from OpenStreetMap.

*   **[CyclOSM](https://www.cyclosm.org/)**: Aims to provide a beautiful and practical map for cyclists, showing detailed infrastructure.
    *   **Open Source**: Yes ([GitHub](https://github.com/cyclosm/cyclosm-cartocss-style/))

*   **[OpenCycleMap](https://www.opencyclemap.org/)**: One of the first global cycling maps based on OSM data.
    *   **Open Source**: No. The service is proprietary, though it uses open data.

*   **[hikebikemap.org](http.hikebikemap.org/)**: A map that renders both hiking and cycling routes.
    *   **Open Source**: Yes. The map style is available under a free license.

*   **[mtbmap.cz](https://mtbmap.cz/)**: A Czech-based map with a focus on mountain biking trails.
    *   **Open Source**: Yes. The style is openly available.

*   **[Organic Maps](https://organicmaps.app/)**: A privacy-focused, offline map application for iOS and Android with routing for cycling.
    *   **Open Source**: Yes (Apache 2.0 License, code on [GitHub](https://github.com/organicmaps/organicmaps)).

*   **[CycleStreets](https://www.cyclestreets.net/)**: A comprehensive cycle journey planner and map, primarily focused on the UK.
    *   **Open Source**: Yes ([GitHub](https://github.com/cyclestreets)).

## 2. Bikeway Design Guides & Resources

These are official publications and standards for designing cycling infrastructure.

*   **[NACTO Urban Bikeway Design Guide](https://nacto.org/publication/urban-bikeway-design-guide/)**: The leading guide for designing urban bikeways in North America, setting standards for protected lanes, intersections, and more.
    *   **Open Source**: No. The guide is a copyrighted publication, though often freely accessible online.

*   **[AASHTO Guide for the Development of Bicycle Facilities](https://bookstore.transportation.org/item_details.aspx?ID=4450)**: A key design guide from the American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials, providing national standards.
    *   **Open Source**: No. This is a paid, copyrighted publication.

*   **[CROW Design Manual for Bicycle Traffic](https://crowplatform.com/product/design-manual-for-bicycle-traffic/)**: The design manual from the Netherlands, widely regarded as the most advanced standard for bicycle infrastructure in the world.
    *   **Open Source**: No. This is a paid, copyrighted publication.

*   **[Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) Bikeway Selection Guide](httpshttps://safety.fhwa.dot.gov/ped_bike/tools_solve/bikeway_selection_guide/)**: A US government resource to help transportation practitioners select the appropriate bikeway type.
    *   **Open Source**: Yes. As a US federal government publication, it is in the public domain.

## 3. Route Planning Tools & Apps

These applications provide route planning, navigation, and tracking for cyclists.

*   **[cycle.travel](https://cycle.travel/)**: An online route planner that prioritizes quiet roads and dedicated cycleways.
    *   **Open Source**: No. The service is proprietary.

*   **[Komoot](https://www.komoot.com/)**: A popular route planner and navigation app for cycling and hiking, with a strong community and collection of routes.
    *   **Open Source**: No. The service is proprietary.

*   **[Bikemap](https://www.bikemap.net/)**: A large collection of user-generated cycle routes and a route planner.
    *   **Open Source**: No. The service is proprietary.

*   **[RouteScout](https://www.routescout.com/)**: Builds scenic and safe routes based on real-world data and user preferences for surface, elevation, etc.
    *   **Open Source**: No. The service is proprietary.

*   **[Naviki](https://www.naviki.org/)**: A comprehensive cycle planner with apps for various platforms, offering routing for different types of cycling (leisure, mountain bike, racing).
    *   **Open Source**: No. The service is proprietary.

*   **[BRouter](http://brouter.de/brouter-web/)**: A highly configurable routing engine, particularly powerful for experienced users who want to fine-tune routing parameters.
    *   **Open Source**: Yes ([GitHub](https://github.com/abrensch/brouter)).
